name: editorial-minimal
description: Apply restrained editorial minimalism for portfolios, content-heavy pages, and calm premium interfaces. Use for newspaper, magazine, document, or warm monochrome directions.
---

Use this preset when the interface should feel like a refined printed system translated to the web.

## Core Mood

- warm or neutral paper-like backgrounds
- strong typographic hierarchy
- clear rules, dividers, captions, and columns
- restrained accent usage
- quiet motion

## Typography

- pair a distinctive editorial display face with a readable serif or refined sans body
- headlines should feel composed, not loud
- labels and metadata may use a precise mono or small caps treatment
- protect reading widths aggressively

## Layout

- think in columns, rails, figures, captions, and section rules
- use panels only when they clarify grouping; do not default to card grids
- prefer vertical flow and narrative sequencing over dashboard-like compartmentalization
- avoid narrow rails that force ugly wrapping

## Color

- one warm accent at most
- off-white or bone backgrounds
- strong text contrast without pure black
- no neon, no generic gradients, no glow

## Motion

- fades and small lifts only
- reduced motion support is mandatory
- motion should feel editorial, not theatrical

## Avoid

- heavy glass or blur
- loud shadows
- bright SaaS accents
- overly symmetrical “template” layouts
- decorative widgets that break the reading experience

Use this preset to make interfaces feel calmer, sharper, and more literate.
