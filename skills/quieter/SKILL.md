Make the design calmer, more legible, and more refined without draining it of identity.

Use this skill when the current UI feels over-designed, too loud, too saturated, too animated, or visually tiring. If the user wants a broader redesign, use `redesign-ui`. If they want a calm editorial direction specifically, use `editorial-minimal`.

## Diagnose the Intensity Source

Look for:

- too many visual ideas competing at once
- saturation or contrast that stays high everywhere
- heavy shadows, blur, or texture that overwhelm content
- typography that is oversized or over-weighted across the board
- motion that performs personality instead of supporting the interface
- boxed or layered surfaces that create clutter rather than structure

Decide what should remain as the anchor and what should recede.

## Quiet the Design Systematically

### Typography

- reduce unnecessary weight and size extremes
- improve line length, spacing, and reading rhythm
- keep one clear anchor instead of several competing headlines

### Color & Surface

- reduce accent spread; let neutrals carry more of the interface
- soften saturation before removing color entirely
- replace heavy chrome with cleaner borders, spacing, and quieter planes

### Layout

- simplify over-compartmentalized sections
- reduce unnecessary overlap, tilt, framing devices, or nested panels
- turn unresolved empty space into intentional breathing room

### Motion

- keep functional motion, remove theatrical motion
- shorten travel distance and reduce visual spectacle
- maintain visible interaction feedback

## Guardrails

- Do not flatten the hierarchy into sameness.
- Do not remove the only source of personality.
- Do not mistake smaller for better.
- Do not keep decorative noise that hurts reading.
- Do not make the UI feel unfinished in the name of minimalism.

## Skill Routing

- `editorial-minimal` for calm reading-heavy or newspaper-like refinement
- `normalize` when the problem is system drift, not intensity
- `polish` for final detail cleanup after the calming pass

Quieting succeeds when the interface feels more controlled, more readable, and more expensive.
