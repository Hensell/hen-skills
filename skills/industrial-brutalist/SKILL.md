name: industrial-brutalist
description: Apply industrial or brutalist UI direction with rigid grids, raw typography, utilitarian color, and mechanical separators. Use for tactical, editorial, data-heavy, or declassified-document aesthetics.
---

Use this preset when the interface should feel engineered, severe, and unmistakably structural.

Pick one mode per project:

- **Swiss industrial print**: high-contrast light background, hard rules, heavy sans, red accent
- **tactical telemetry**: dark terminal-like surface, dense mono data, rigid framing devices

Do not mix both in the same interface.

## Typography

- macro typography should feel structural and uncompromising
- mono is for telemetry, metadata, coordinates, labels
- serif is optional and rare; use only for deliberate contrast

## Layout

- strict grids, visible separators, and compartmentalization
- zero soft corners by default
- asymmetry is fine, but only when it still feels engineered
- use density strategically: packed data zones against controlled empty space

## Color

- one dominant substrate and one harsh accent
- hazard red is usually enough
- no gradients, no candy colors, no soft ambient glows

## Effects

- texture may come from halftone, scanlines, bitmap treatment, or paper noise
- effects must support the concept, not become decoration

## Guardrails

- do not use this style for products that need warmth or softness
- do not let the concept break readability
- do not fake complexity with random strings or noise if the structure is weak

This preset is for raw confidence, not chaos.
